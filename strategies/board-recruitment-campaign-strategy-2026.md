# BLKOUT Board Recruitment Campaign Strategy
## 3-Week Comprehensive Plan | January 27 - February 14, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

**Campaign Goal:** Recruit 5 qualified board members for BLKOUT by February 14th, 2026

**Target Positions:**
1. Chair
2. Treasurer
3. Secretary
4. Technology Director
5. Community Director

**Application Target:** 25+ total applications (minimum 5 per position)

**Campaign Duration:** 19 days (January 27 - February 14, 2026)

**Voice:** IVOR Cummings - Professional yet warm, inviting participation in governance

---

## Target Audience Profiles

### Audience 1: Black Professionals in Finance/Law/Tech
**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-50
- Location: London, Manchester, Birmingham, Bristol
- Career level: Mid-senior professionals (5+ years experience)
- Income: Professional salary brackets

**Motivations:**
- Seeking board/governance experience for CV
- Want to give back to community while using professional skills
- Interest in social enterprise/ethical business
- Looking for networking opportunities with purpose

**Platform Behavior:**
- Heavy LinkedIn users (daily)
- Instagram for personal/cultural content
- Twitter for news and professional discourse
- Newsletter readers (subscribe to industry updates)

**Messaging Hooks:**
- "Build your governance portfolio"
- "Apply your expertise to liberation technology"
- "Join a pioneering social enterprise board"

---

### Audience 2: Community Organizers Seeking Governance Experience
**Demographics:**
- Age: 25-45
- Deep community ties (often volunteers, activists)
- May work in third sector, education, or freelance
- Strong social media presence

**Motivations:**
- Transition from grassroots to institutional leadership
- Want to formalize community work
- Seeking recognition and development opportunities
- Desire to influence at governance level

**Platform Behavior:**
- Instagram-heavy (stories, reels)
- Twitter for discourse and mobilization
- Less LinkedIn presence
- WhatsApp/community group members

**Messaging Hooks:**
- "Your community knowledge IS governance expertise"
- "From organizing to governing"
- "Lived experience valued and needed"

---

### Audience 3: LGBTQ+ Professionals in Corporate/Charity Sectors
**Demographics:**
- Age: 30-55
- Working in corporate diversity roles, charity sector, public sector
- Often have existing board/trustee experience
- Broad professional networks

**Motivations:**
- Intersectional representation opportunities
- LGBTQ+-specific governance experience
- Tech/innovation focus appeals
- Networking with other LGBTQ+ leaders

**Platform Behavior:**
- LinkedIn primary (professional content)
- Twitter for LGBTQ+ discourse
- Instagram for community connection
- Industry newsletter subscribers

**Messaging Hooks:**
- "Govern at the intersection"
- "LGBTQ+ leadership in tech for good"
- "Build institutions that serve us"

## Engagement Tactics

### Response Templates

#### For "I'm interested but not sure I'm qualified"
```
Dear [name], thank you for your interest. Many excellent board members started with exactly that feeling. We provide comprehensive training and support - your passion and perspective are the qualifications we value most. Would you like to chat informally about the role? Drop us a line at governance@blkoutuk.com - no commitment, just conversation. The revolution needs people like you.
```

#### For "What's the time commitment really like?"
```
Great question! Board members commit 4-6 hours monthly on average - that's quarterly meetings (2-3 hours each), preparation time, and occasional events. We're mindful of busy lives and work flexibly. Many of our community members balance board service with demanding careers. We make it work because the work matters.
```

#### For "Can I apply if I don't have board experience?"
```
Absolutely! We're specifically looking for people who bring fresh perspectives. We provide full onboarding and ongoing training. Your professional skills, community connections, or lived experience ARE relevant experience. Don't let imposter syndrome stop you - we believe in your potential.
```

#### For "Is lived experience required?"
```
Lived experience as a Black queer person is strongly valued but not required for all positions. We believe in building a diverse board with different perspectives. What matters most is commitment to our mission and community. If you're passionate about Black queer liberation, we want to hear from you.
```

#### For position-specific questions
```
Thanks for asking about the [Position] role. This position focuses on [key responsibility]. Ideal candidates have [key skills], but we value potential as much as experience. Would you like more details? Check our full position description at blkoutuk.com/governance or email governance@blkoutuk.com with specific questions.
```

## Risk Mitigation

### Low Application Volume
**Trigger:** Less than 5 applications by February 7th
**Response:**
1. Increase posting frequency
2. Activate personal outreach immediately
3. Request partner organizations for direct emails
4. Consider paid promotion on LinkedIn
5. Extend deadline by 1 week if necessary

### Low Engagement
**Trigger:** Engagement rate below 2% for 3+ consecutive posts
**Response:**
1. Review content for accessibility and clarity
2. Test different posting times
3. Increase story content (higher engagement)
4. Add more visual content, less text
5. Use more questions and calls to action

### Negative Feedback
**Trigger:** Critical comments or concerns raised
**Response:**
1. Respond promptly and professionally
2. Take valid concerns offline for detailed response
3. Address systemic issues publicly if relevant
4. Document feedback for organizational learning
5. Do not delete criticism unless abusive

### Platform Issues
**Trigger:** Account restriction, algorithm changes, or technical problems
**Response:**
1. Shift focus to unaffected platforms
2. Increase newsletter reliance
3. Activate partner amplification
4. Use personal accounts for sharing
5. Email list direct outreach
