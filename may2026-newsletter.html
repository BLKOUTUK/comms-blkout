A digital commons for Black queer organising?
Here's something we've been chewing over. Black queer organisations across the UK keep solving the same problems separately — events, mailing lists, member directories, payments, archives. Each from scratch. Each on rented ground. And the tools we end up renting rarely belong to people who share our interests.
We've started early conversations with peer organisations — including BBJC, Black Thrive and DiaspoRainbow — about what a shared digital commons could look like. Tools we own together. Infrastructure that doesn't disappear when a grant does. Decisions made by the people the tools actually serve.
More on all this over the next few weeks. For now — if you run, sit on the board of, or work inside a Black-led, queer-led, or Black queer organisation in the UK, and this question is one you've been turning over too, we'd love to hear from you. rob@blkoutuk.com.
 
Critical Frequency — the campaign continues
Last month we shared the case we'd been making at the BMHWA: that there's no reliable evidence base on mental health outcomes for Black queer men in the UK, and that this isn't a measurement problem — it's a political one. You can't commission for people the system can't see. Since then, we've turned the argument into a campaign.
Critical Frequency is the home of it. The website — critical.blkoutuk.com — carries the case, the numbers, the explainer video, and a remix-this kit you're welcome to grab, adapt, or rebuild from. Three asks: count us, fund the research, share the infrastructure to do it.
And we're actively looking for partners — in mental health, public health, data, research, commissioning, community development. If that's your patch, drop a line to rob@blkoutuk.com.

Join the board: Finance & Fundraising — deadline 31 May
BLKOUT is a Community Benefit Society. The members own it. The board steers it. Every function on our board works in pairs — for accountability, mutual support, and so the work doesn't stall when life happens to one person. It's a model we're proud of, and it's one we'd love more of you to be part of.
We're recruiting two people to form our Finance & Fundraising pair. You don't need to be a qualified accountant. You need to care about this community, want it to thrive, and be willing to learn alongside the rest of us. If you've raised money, kept books, sat on a treasury, run a small organisation's finances, or simply spent enough time around the financial side of community work to have opinions — we'd like to hear from you.
