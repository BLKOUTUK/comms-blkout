# reframe-B-whats-mistuned

**Audience: Profile B — Arts, culture, and heritage funders**

---

The metaphor in our name is precise: in physics, critical frequency is the threshold at which a signal breaks through interference and travels further [1]. Currently, the UK’s institutional architecture is broadcasting on the wrong frequency for Black queer reality [1]. You might reasonably ask how a mental health proposition fits into an arts and heritage portfolio. The answer is that these separated sectors are the system's, not the community's [2]. As our *Seven Lives* lineage demonstrates directly, cultural recognition is psychic medicine; the cultural memory work is itself the prevention work [3, 4].

Three structural frequencies are currently mistuned:

**The diagnostic frequency is mistuned.** Standard institutional frameworks read the compounded weight of anti-Black racism and anti-queer prejudice as individual pathology [5]. The diagnostic instrument cannot pick up the signal of a life lived at that intersection [5]. Reframing this requires acknowledging that we are addressing a crisis of systemic erasure, necessitating a deep cultural memory infrastructure for a profoundly under-archived community [6, 7]. 

**The evidence frequency is mistuned.** Historically, research and cultural documentation have extracted from the communities they claim to serve, cataloguing people as evidence in scholarship written elsewhere [8]. To retune this, our practice is community-led from the start, rather than merely designed with community input [9, 10]. We are building the Commons as openly-licensed sector property, anchored by a CBS asset-lock that forms a permanent, structural commitment to non-extraction [6, 11, 12]. 

**The intervention frequency is mistuned.** Traditional services intervene on isolated individuals, but the mechanism of change in Black queer life is deeply networked [13, 14]. Retuning this broadcast requires a single cornerstone partner for a £4–6m founding partnership to underwrite our architecture [1, 15]. We name this investment explicitly as reparations under UN General Assembly Resolution 60/147, rather than impact philanthropy or social investment [7]. Together, we will hold our genuine uncertainties—network-positioned therapy as novel, community-led data stewardship as unproven, and AI within community trust as uncharted—not as risks to be mitigated, but as strengths held within a rigorous action-learning frame, rather than a traditional steering-group structure [16, 17].
