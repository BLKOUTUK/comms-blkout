You inform us that network-positioned therapy is unproven, and suggest we might instead pursue something more "established." This is a fascinating request. The "established" clinical methodology in this country yields a 3.8 per cent therapy completion rate for Black patients, functioning alongside a coercive apparatus that spends £12–16 million annually to detain an estimated 150 to 200 Black queer men under the Mental Health Act [1-3]. It is ridiculous to demand we repeat a systemic failure simply because it has been rigorously documented. As Berto Pasuka once remarked to his own critics, would you say ice-cream is no good because it is not cheese? [4]

You ask for tested certainty where none honestly exists. We will not soften our reality to soothe your institutional anxiety. Network-positioned therapy is a genuinely novel concept [5]. Community-led data stewardship at this scale is unproven [5]. The exact topography of artificial intelligence intersecting with community trust remains entirely uncharted territory [5]. We hold these genuine uncertainties not as risks to be mitigated, but as our primary methodological strengths [5]. This is precisely why we require our single £4–6 million cornerstone partner to join us in a transparent action-learning frame, rather than retreating into the risk-averse bureaucracy of a conventional steering-group structure [6, 7]. We have no interest in assembling a panel of contributing funders to dilute the necessary analytical seriousness [6, 8].

Our architecture is community-led from the start—a stance we will never dilute into being merely "designed with community input." Secured within a Community Benefit Society holding a permanent asset-lock, this is our structural commitment to absolute non-extraction [9]. Make no mistake: we are not offering a polite exercise in impact philanthropy. We are executing a mandate of reparations under UN General Assembly Resolution 60/147, building the infrastructure the state was obligated to build, but did not [10, 11]. 

If you wish to fund what is established, you are welcome to continue subsidising the crisis. If you wish to fund transformation, bring your conviction to the unknown.
