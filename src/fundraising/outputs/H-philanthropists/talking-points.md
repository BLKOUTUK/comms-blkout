**Opening Hook**
We are not proposing a new clinical service, but rather the structural apparatus by which a community that is already quietly healing itself can be resourced to do so at the scale the crisis demands [1, 2].

**Three Landing Claims**
* Invisible to standard diagnostic and evidence frequencies, Black queer men are operating as a hidden healthcare workforce to prevent severe crises that would otherwise cost the NHS millions [2-4].
* Rather than treating isolated individuals within a failing system, our architecture uses operational network science—specifically complex contagion—to spread new collective norms of wellness across the community [5-7].
* Grounded in UN General Assembly Resolution 60/147, this work is explicitly an act of institutional restitution and debt repayment for colonialism's ongoing violence, not an exercise in impact philanthropy [8, 9].

**Anchors of Credibility**
* **A Ten-Year Track Record:** Our operational seriousness is proven by a decade of trust-based practice, which includes building the UK's largest network for Black queer men, delivering complex public funding, and cultivating standing relationships with senior public health bodies [10-12].
* **The CBS Asset-Lock:** To guarantee this infrastructure remains community-led from the start, BLKOUT Creative Ltd is registered as a Community Benefit Society holding a permanent, unalterable asset-lock; it is structurally impossible for this investment to be captured or accumulated as private wealth [13, 14].

**The Ask**
* I am seeking a single cornerstone partner with the analytical seriousness to commit £4–6 million over five to seven years, rather than a panel of contributing funders, to decisively underwrite the integration of this architecture [15, 16].

**An Open Invitation**
* I invite you to walk alongside us in a rigorous action-learning frame—eschewing conventional steering-group structures for genuine participation—where we will hold genuine uncertainties, such as the uncharted territory of network-positioned therapy and community-led data stewardship, not as risks but as our greatest methodological strengths [16-18].
