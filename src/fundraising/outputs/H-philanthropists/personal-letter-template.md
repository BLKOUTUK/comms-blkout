Dear [INSERT NAME],

For the past decade, I have been building BLKOUT to connect and support Black queer men in the UK [1]. Our community operates as a hidden healthcare workforce, quietly managing crises that receive no systemic investment, while the NHS spends £12–16 million annually detaining 150-200 of our brothers under the Mental Health Act [2, 3]. One year of this coercive crisis response costs more than the entire five-year prevention infrastructure we are now ready to build [3, 4]. 

I am writing to you because I am seeking a single cornerstone partner to underwrite this infrastructure with a commitment of £4–6 million over five to seven years [5]. I am deliberately not looking to assemble a panel of contributing funders [5]. Complex systems change requires the analytical seriousness and unified patience of a single anchor to catalyse the wider resourcing landscape [5, 6]. 

I want to be unequivocally clear about what this investment represents [7]. Grounded in UN General Assembly Resolution 60/147, this is an act of institutional restitution for colonialism’s ongoing violence, racialised policing, and systemic clinical neglect [7]. I refuse to soften this reality into the palatable language of "impact philanthropy" or "social investment" [7, 8]. This is debt repayment—building the infrastructure the state was obligated to build, but did not [7, 8].

If you are asking yourself, "Can I trust BLKOUT with a commitment at this scale?", I understand. My answer lies in our ten-year track record of delivery, not in a charismatic pitch [9]. We have built the UK's largest network for this community [1]. Furthermore, in November 2025, we secured this work in a durable cooperative legal home as a Community Benefit Society (CBS) holding a permanent, unalterable asset-lock [10, 11]. This is our structural commitment to non-extraction: it is legally impossible for this investment to be captured as private wealth [10]. We are community-led from the start—which we will never soften into merely being "designed with community input"—with authority deliberately distributed to a paid Advisory Circle of peer organisers [12].

The partnership begins with a £250,000–£500,000 Foundation Year [13]. This is not a preparatory phase, but the concrete construction of the trust and operational tooling upon which full delivery entirely depends [14, 15]. It unlocks the hiring of our Programme Director, the recruitment of culturally competent therapists, and the technical specification of our shared digital platforms [13, 15, 16]. By the close of Year 1, the architecture will be fully operationalised, allowing your cornerstone commitment to securely underwrite Years 2–5, where we roll out 1,825 network-positioned therapy interventions, dispatch embedded systems change leaders, and launch our deliberative assemblies [16-19].

We are not looking for a transactional funder [20]. I invite you to participate alongside our Advisory Circle in a rigorous action-learning frame [21]. We will not convert this into a conventional, risk-averse steering group [12, 21]. We want you in the room as we transparently examine the work, holding our genuine uncertainties—the novelty of network-positioned therapy, the unproven scale of community-led data stewardship, and the uncharted territory of AI and community trust—not as risks to be mitigated, but as our greatest methodological strengths [21, 22].

If you trust communities to lead their own transformation and understand reparations as a structural commitment, I would like to arrange a 45-minute conversation to explore what we can build together [23, 24]. 

Warmly,

Rob Berkeley MBE  
Founder & Director, BLKOUT Creative Ltd
