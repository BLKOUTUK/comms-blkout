# founder-dependency-response

---

When evaluating a foundational investment, addressing the risk of founder-dependency is strictly necessary. There is a well-documented historical pattern of Black-led organisations collapsing after a charismatic founder departs. It is vital to be candid about this reality without flattening the cause into an individual failing: this fragility is typically the result of systemic underfunding, which forces organisations to rely on a single charismatic leader to secure scarce capital. When that leader transitions, the institutional memory and relational trust frequently exit with them. BLKOUT is explicitly engineered to prevent this by replacing charismatic dependency with robust, distributed infrastructure.

In November 2025, after a decade of community organising, we secured our practice within a durable cooperative legal home: BLKOUT Creative Ltd, a Community Benefit Society (CBS) registered with the Financial Conduct Authority [1-3]. Legal governance does not sit with a single founder; it is held by a cooperative board of seven Black queer-identified directors, accountable to more than 200 active members who hold standing voting rights [2, 4]. Crucially, this CBS structure carries a permanent, unalterable asset-lock [2]. This is our structural commitment to non-extraction: every penny of revenue is legally bound to community reinvestment, making it structurally impossible for the organisation’s wealth to be captured for private gain, regardless of who is at the helm [2]. 

Operational authority is deliberately distributed beyond the core organisation. We have established an Advisory Circle: a standing body of community organisers drawn from across the UK Black queer and adjacent structurally excluded movements [5, 6]. Paid honoraria for their expertise, this circle holds real, independent authority over our action-learning curriculum and the ongoing evolution of our practice [5, 6]. By permanently distributing authority, we guarantee that the organisation remains community-led from the start, a structural reality we categorically refuse to soften into merely being "designed with community input" [7]. 

Furthermore, the methodology of Critical Frequency is built to be inherited. Through 'The Commons', our cooperative governance templates, fair-use frameworks, and archive protocols are openly licensed [6, 8]. This ensures that the structural apparatus outlasts any single leader and becomes shared sector property [7, 8]. Even our approach to holding genuine uncertainties—such as the novelty of network-positioned therapy, the unproven scale of community-led data stewardship, and the uncharted territory of artificial intelligence and community trust—relies on collective inquiry within an action-learning frame [9, 10]. We strictly will not convert this transparent practice into a conventional, risk-averse steering-group structure. 

To underwrite this integrated architecture, we are seeking a single cornerstone partner to commit £4–6 million over five to seven years [11, 12]. We will not assemble a panel of contributing funders to meet this need. Grounded in UN General Assembly Resolution 60/147, this investment is an act of reparations and debt repayment, not impact philanthropy [13]. Your investment does not buy a charismatic founder’s vision; it funds the concrete construction of a distributed infrastructure that a community already healing itself can inherit, share, and sustain for decades [14, 15].
