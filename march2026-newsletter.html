Call for Participants

Ivor’s Compass — Writers Wanted

Heritage • Writing Workshop • Croydon • April 2026

We’re looking for 12 writers to join a full-day workshop exploring the life of Ivor Cummings OBE — the gay father of the Windrush generation, Croydon native, and a man whose story was invisible for thirty years.

Too queer for Windrush narratives. Too Black for LGBTQ+ histories. Too complex for simple categories. His story is a compass for navigating similar waters today.

Each writer will create one short meditation inspired by an episode from Ivor’s life. These become part of a digital wellness journal — heritage you can use, not just observe. A commissioned poet will write a 3-minute spoken word piece, animated as a short film in the styles of Black British queer artists.

What we’re offering:

• £125 commission fee per writer
• Full-day facilitated workshop
• Research materials and support provided
• Your work published in the Ivor’s Compass digital journal
• No prior writing experience required — lived experience is the qualification

Funded by the Samuel Coleridge-Taylor 150 Small Heritage Grant from Croydon Culture Service. Ivor was a distant cousin of Coleridge-Taylor through Sierra Leonean kinship networks — the connection that brings this story home.
